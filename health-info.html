Understanding Heart Health

Heart health is essential to maintaining overall well-being. The heart is a powerful muscle responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. Taking good care of your heart can prevent heart diseases like heart attack, stroke, and coronary artery disease. It is important to maintain a balanced diet, exercise regularly, and avoid stress to keep your heart healthy.
A heart health report can include a cardiovascular health score, blood pressure, and other information about your heart health:
Cardiovascular health score

Blood pressure
A healthy blood pressure is 120/80, and 140/90 is normal for most people. Higher blood pressure readings can increase the risk of heart attack or stroke. Risk assessment
A healthcare provider can use a risk calculator to estimate your risk of heart attack, stroke, or death from heart or blood vessel disease. Risk factors include high blood pressure, cholesterol, age, sex, race, smoking, and medications.

Heart Attack Signs & Safety Measures to Prevent Heart Disease

Heart Attack Warning Signs

Symptoms can vary from person to person like Chest Pain, Shortness of breath,Lightheadedness or dizziness and some people may have no symptoms. If you think someone might be having a heart attack, call emergency services immediately
A heart attack can have many warning signs, including:
Chest pain: The most common symptom, chest pain can feel like pressure, tightness, squeezing, or aching in the center or left side of the chest. The pain may come and go.
Pain in other parts of the body: Pain can spread from the chest to the arms, jaw, neck, back, or upper belly.
Shortness of breath: You might gasp for breath or feel like you need to take in deep breaths.
Sweating: You might break out into a sweat for no reason.
Feeling sick: You might feel sick to your stomach or vomit.
Lightheadedness or dizziness: You might feel dizzy or like you might pass out.

Eat a Healthy Diet

A heart-healthy diet is rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed foods and limit your salt and sugar intake and can help protect your heart and reduce your risk of heart attack, stroke, and other health issues.
Some benefits of a heart-healthy diet include:
Improved cholesterol: A heart-healthy diet can help you reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) and increase good cholesterol (HDL).
Better blood pressure: A heart-healthy diet can help you manage your blood pressure.
Reduced risk of type 2 diabetes: A heart-healthy diet can help lower your risk of type 2 diabetes.
Reduced risk of heart rhythm disorders: A heart-healthy diet can help reduce your risk of heart rhythm disorders

Stay Hydrated

Staying hydrated can help your heart by making it easier to pump blood throughout your body. Dehydration can cause your blood to thicken, which can increase the risk of heart attacks and blood clots
Heart function
When you're hydrated, your heart can pump blood more efficiently through your blood vessels to your muscles. This helps your muscles work more effectively.
Electrolyte balance
Your heart relies on a balance of electrolytes like sodium, potassium, and magnesium to keep its rhythm steady. Without enough electrolytes, you may experience irregular heartbeats
Here are some tips to stay hydrated:
Add some lemon or fruit to your water
Set reminders to drink water regularly
Limit your salt intake
Avoid sugary drinks

Exercise Regularly

Regular physical activity strengthens the heart, improves circulation, and helps control weight. Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise daily.
Regular exercise has many benefits for your heart, including:
Lowering risk factors
Exercise can reduce your risk of heart disease by lowering blood pressure, cholesterol, and triglyceride levels. It can also help you maintain a healthy weight, which is a major risk factor for heart disease.
Improving circulation
Exercise strengthens your heart and improves circulation, which increases oxygen levels in your body.
Reducing inflammation
Exercise can reduce C-reactive protein (CRP), a sign of inflammation and an increased risk of heart disease.
Reducing stress
Exercise can help reduce stress, which may contribute to other risk factors for heart disease.

Key Symptoms to Look Out For

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat
  • Lightheadedness
  • Fatigue
